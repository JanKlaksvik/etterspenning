Strand anchors

Strands are the most common solution for rock anchors and post-tensioning work where high capacity, robust load transfer and field flexibility are required. Below is a simple overview of typical cross-sectional area and breaking load.

Type Area (mm²) Breaking load (kN)
Normal 140 260.4
Super 150 279
Compact 165 306.9
Values are provided as practical reference and must always be verified against the manufacturer documentation (certificate/data sheet) for the selected system.

Permanent anchors

There are strict requirements for production, materials, equipment, grout and crew experience. Here is a short summary.

Laws and regulations: In Norway, System 1+ is required for production, and EAD requirements have been introduced, but most manufacturers do not yet have this. Anchor construction is described in NS EN 1537:2013. Grout testing and annual testing are described in NS EN 445, 446 and 447. Competence requirements are described in NS EN 1537:2013. Requirements for stressing and equipment are described in NS EN ISO 22477-5:2018.

Temporary anchors

The same requirements as for permanent anchors apply, except for corrugated plastic ducting along the full anchor length.

Differentiated anchoring

Differentiated anchoring for rock anchors represents a technically improved solution compared with conventional anchoring. The cost level for both types is approximately the same. This anchoring type has been used on several ferry quays in Norway since 2012 where tensile anchoring has been required.
