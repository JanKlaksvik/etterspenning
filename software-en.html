Why software for post-tensioning work?

In practice, deviations often occur in the handoff between planning and execution: unclear drawings, wrong field data, missing traceability, or protocols completed too late. Properly designed software can:

  • enforce minimum data (so a protocol is actually complete),
  • reduce manual errors (format, units, rounding),
  • provide better history (who did what and when),
  • make handover easier (PDF/report with attachments).
Grouting
EN 445/446/447/206
Registration of project data, borehole and length data, mixing (water amount/time), parameters, and testing. The goal is stable quality and a traceable log that can be exported.
  • Project data: name, number, anchor count, type (bar/strand)
  • Borehole/geometry data: lengths/diameters, steel casing/liners
  • Log: temperature, consistency, time, volume/pressure (where relevant)
Stressing / protocol
Execution
Stressing protocol according to standards, linking force, steps, elongation, and checkpoints. Data can be imported from the basis and completed in the field.
  • Stepwise loading and acceptance limits
  • ΔL measurements and deviation log
  • Calibration reference (jack/gauge/measuring tool)
  • Jack selection (library) + QR verification on site
Competence and training
Low threshold
Short lessons and tests that support internal competence growth without presenting it as certification.
  • Tasks and checkpoints per role
  • Short tests (4 answer options) and history
  • Can be linked to projects/equipment (relevant training)
Deviation and improvement
Audit trail
A simple way to register field deviations (text/image), notify responsible parties, and follow up actions.
  • Register an event with image + short description
  • Notify admin/quality responsible
  • Status, responsibility, action, and closure
