Post-tensioning - precise language, precise execution

In Norwegian, the term "spennarmering" is often used, but for post-tensioning, "etterspenning" is more precise in practice: the element is tensioned after the structure is established. This page gathers execution-focused information, data and tools that support quality, traceability and efficient delivery, while meeting legal, standard and guidance requirements.

What it takes to deliver an anchor - from requirements to final documentation

A structured process reduces deviations, time and errors
1) Prosjekteringsgrunnlag og krav Requirements / standard

Define load, geometry, environment, corrosion protection, test program (stressing), acceptance criteria and creep where relevant. Clarify quantities, lengths, traceability, documentation and responsibilities between design and site execution.

2) Production and delivery Material / QA

Verify production (System 1+/EAD), material certificates, component identification, marking and delivery package. Plan transport, handling, storage and protection before installation.

3) Installation and assembly Execution

Borehole data, hole geometry, cleaning, positioning and centralizing. Correct assembly of heads/anchors, ducts, sealing and transition zones. Everything that can affect grouting and later stressing. Most errors happen here.

4) Grouting and control Grout / control / test

Test before start and during grouting, use correct mix design, and log quantity/time, consistency, temperature, pressure and volume. Document grouting, tests and any deviations.

5) Stressing/control and final documentation Stressing / protocol

Check calibration certificates, stressing procedure (acceptance, suitability and/or investigation testing), elongation measurements, acceptance criteria/creep, calibration and traceability. Finish with complete protocol, control points and report according to NS EN ISO 22477-5.
